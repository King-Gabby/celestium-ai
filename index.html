Featured Project

Malaria Detection System

A YOLOv8-based computer vision system trained on the BBBC041 dataset to detect and classify P. vivax parasite lifecycle stages in thick blood smear microscopy images — assisting clinical diagnostics in settings where trained microscopists are scarce.

The model classifies four infected cell stages — Ring, Trophozoite, Schizont, and Gametocyte — alongside healthy red blood cells, generating bounding boxes, confidence scores, and per-image diagnostic summaries.

Our Vision

Africa's future will be shaped by intelligent systems built for African realities

For too long, AI infrastructure has been built for problems elsewhere — then adapted, often poorly, for African contexts. CelesTium AI takes the opposite approach: we start with African environments, African datasets, and African constraints.

Our mission is to develop computer vision infrastructure that materially improves healthcare delivery, transportation safety, and critical services across the continent — not as a future aspiration, but as work already in progress.

01

Healthcare Diagnostics

AI systems that give clinicians in under-resourced settings access to machine-precision diagnostic assistance — starting with malaria, expanding across infectious diseases.

02

Transportation Intelligence

Computer vision for African roads — where road markings fade, vehicles vary widely, and formal infrastructure is limited. Built for the actual environment.

03

AI Infrastructure

The datasets, APIs, and deployment tools that Africa's AI ecosystem needs to build on. Not just applications — the underlying layer that others can build upon.
